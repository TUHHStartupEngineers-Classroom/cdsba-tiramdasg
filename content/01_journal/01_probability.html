Assignment 3

Given:

  • P(A) = 0.04
  • P(B|A) = 0.97
  • P(B|A) = 0.01

To find P(A|B) and P(A|B)

  1. Calculate P(B): \[ P(B) = P(B|A) \cdot P(A) + P(B|\overline{A}) \cdot P(\overline{A}) \]

\[ P(B) = 0.97 \cdot 0.04 + 0.01 \cdot (1 - 0.04) \\ \]

\[ P(B) = 0.0484 \] 2. Apply Bayes’ Theorem:

\[ P(A | B) = \frac{P(B | A) \cdot P(A)}{P(B)} \\ \]

\[ P (A | B) = \frac{0.97 \cdot 0.04}{0.0484}\\ \]

\[ P(A | B) = 0.791 \] Similarly, \[ P(\overline{A} | B) = \frac{P(B | \overline{A}) \cdot P(\overline{A})}{P(B)} \\ \] \[ P (\overline{A} | B) = \frac{0.01 \cdot (1 - 0.04)}{0.0484}\\ \] \[ P(\overline{A} | B) = 0.209 \]
